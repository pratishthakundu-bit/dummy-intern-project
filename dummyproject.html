About Acme

Who We Are

Acme Studios is an independent brand and design agency founded in 2014. We help mid-to-large organisations rethink how their brand shows up in the world — across identity, digital products, and campaigns.

We work with three core principles: clarity over cleverness, craft over speed, and curiosity over assumption. Our team of 64 spans three offices — London, Berlin, and Mumbai.

Departments

The Brand Design Team

The Brand Design team owns visual identity work — logos, type systems, design languages, and brand guidelines. Currently 14 people, led by Marcus Lin. The team sits between Strategy (who frame the problem) and Production (who scale the output).

Junior designers work in pairs with a Senior on each project. Reviews happen twice a week — Mondays for direction, Thursdays for craft.

Departments

The Strategy Team

Strategy is our research and positioning arm. They run discovery, audience interviews, market analysis, and write the brief that design teams build from. Led by Aisha Patel.

How We Work

From Brief to Launch — Our Project Flow

Every project at Acme follows a four-stage flow: Discover → Define → Design → Deliver.

Discover (1–2 weeks): research, interviews, audits. Define (1 week): strategic framing, brand brief. Design (4–8 weeks): concept and craft. Deliver (1–2 weeks): handoff, guidelines, launch support.

How We Work

Our Creative Review Process

Reviews are not approval gates — they are conversations. The presenter shares context, then their thinking, then the work. Reviewers respond with what's working, what isn't, and what's still unclear. The goal is forward motion, not consensus.

Glossary

Acme Terminology

BVG — Brand Voice Guidelines. DTU — Design Team Update (weekly). CR — Creative Review. P-doc — Project Document. Anchor — the central organising idea of a project.

The Northwind Brand Refresh

Project Phase
Deadline · July 28, 2026 · 6 weeks from start of project phase

The Brief

Northwind is a 12-year-old sustainable fashion brand based in Manchester. They've grown from a 3-person studio into a 40-person operation with stockists in 11 countries. Their current visual identity hasn't aged with them — it still feels like a small studio, not a confident, modern brand.

Your Challenge

Develop a refreshed visual direction for Northwind that respects their craft-led heritage but signals their growing maturity. This is not a full identity build — it's a directional exploration.

What to Deliver

  • A short written rationale (300–500 words) explaining your strategic direction
  • 3 visual mood directions — each with a name, a few sample compositions, and a one-line ethos
  • One direction developed further: logo treatment, type pairing, color system, 2–3 sample applications
  • A short reflection (~200 words) on what you'd want to test next if you had more time

What We're Looking For

  • Clarity of thinking — we want to understand why, not just what
  • Craft and care — taste in type, color, and composition
  • Confidence to make a choice and stand behind it
  • Curiosity — does your thinking show you genuinely engaged with Northwind?

Resources

  • Northwind website + brand archive (linked in Knowledge Bank)
  • Sustainable fashion category audit (Strategy team, May 2026)
  • Your mentor — book a check-in any time

Areas to develop further

  • Direction 1's logo treatment could benefit from one more round — the current mark reads as small-studio more than the mature brand the brief asks for.
  • The sample applications are strong as static compositions, but it's hard to imagine them in motion or at small scale. Consider how the system flexes.
One actionable tip
Try the "squint test" on each of your three directions. From across the room or with your eyes half-closed, do they look meaningfully different from each other? If not, that's a signal to push the contrast between options further — it's often where the bravest design choices live.
