The Company

What is WPP?

WPP is the world's largest marketing and communications services group, headquartered in London and operating in over 100 countries. It was founded in 1985 and today employs roughly 100,000 people worldwide.

WPP helps the world's biggest brands grow through advertising, media, public relations, branding, data, and commerce. Rather than being one single company, WPP is a group made up of many specialist agencies — each with its own expertise.

Our Agencies

The WPP Agencies — Who Does What

WPP's agencies are grouped by what they specialise in:

  • WPP Creative — Ogilvy (advertising, PR, health), VML (brand & commerce), AKQA (digital experience), Grey, Landor (brand consulting).
  • WPP Media (GroupM) — Mindshare, Wavemaker, EssenceMediacom — media planning and buying.
  • Burson — global public relations and communications.
  • Hogarth — global content production at scale.
Our Agencies

GroupM & WPP Media — Where You Sit

GroupM is WPP's media investment arm, managing over $60 billion in client media spend globally — making it the largest media buyer in the world. It's now operating as part of WPP Media under a single, unified model.

Its client-facing brands include Mindshare, Wavemaker, and EssenceMediacom. Two specialist engines power the work: GroupM Nexus (performance marketing) and Choreograph (data & technology).

Our Team

How Our Data & Analytics Team Works

Our team turns raw campaign and audience data into clear insights that guide client decisions. We sit between the media planners (who decide where ads run) and the clients (who want to know if it worked).

A typical week involves pulling data from platforms like GA4 and DV360, building dashboards in Tableau, and translating the numbers into a story for client reviews. Interns usually start by supporting reporting and gradually take on their own analysis.

Our Team

How a Campaign Works — Brief to Results

Every campaign follows a familiar arc: Brief → Plan → Launch → Measure → Optimise.

The client shares a brief (their goal). We build a media plan (where & how to reach the audience). The campaign goes live. Then our team measures performance against KPIs and recommends optimisations to improve results over time.

Tools & Dashboards

Key Tools & Dashboards We Use

  • Google Analytics 4 (GA4) — website & campaign behaviour data.
  • DV360 (Display & Video 360) — buying and managing display/video ads.
  • Tableau — building dashboards and visualising data.
  • Nielsen / Comscore — audience measurement and reach data.
Our Team

How to Read a Client Brief

A brief tells you what the client wants to achieve. Read it looking for three things: the objective (what success looks like), the audience (who we're trying to reach), and the constraints (budget, timing, channels). If any of these are unclear, that's a great question to raise.

Glossary

Media & Marketing Glossary

CPM — Cost Per Mille (cost per 1,000 impressions). CTR — Click-Through Rate. ROAS — Return On Ad Spend. SOV — Share of Voice. KPI — Key Performance Indicator. Impression — one view of an ad. Reach — number of unique people who saw it. Frequency — how often each person saw it.

Mini Campaign Performance Analysis

Project Phase
Begins after your Learning Phase · ~3 weeks · Soft deadline 18 July 2026

What We're Looking For

This project is your chance to put everything from your first two weeks into practice. We're not looking for perfection — we're looking for clear thinking, curiosity, and the ability to turn data into a simple story. Treat it as a safe space to learn by doing.

The Task

You'll be given a sample dataset from a (anonymised) past campaign. Your job is to analyse the performance and present what you found — what worked, what didn't, and what you'd recommend doing next time.

What to Deliver

  • A short dashboard or visual summary of the campaign's key metrics (Tableau, Sheets, or Excel — your choice)
  • A 5–8 slide deck walking through your findings and 2–3 recommendations
  • A one-paragraph reflection on what you learned and what you'd explore with more time

What We Value (the attributes that matter)

Clarity over complexitySimple, clear insight beats a fancy chart no one understands.
CuriosityShow us you dug a little deeper than the obvious numbers.
A point of viewDon't just report — make a recommendation and stand behind it.
StorytellingWalk us through it like you're explaining it to a client.
